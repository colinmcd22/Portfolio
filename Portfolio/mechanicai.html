MechanicAI is an end-to-end, mobile mechanic that can monitor and track your car's performance to let guide you in fixing any problems you may have. Using sensor technology, our software is able to analyze specific parts of your car to make sure you are fully aware of the current status of your vehicle. Additionally, MechanicAI allows you to find and book a mechanic near you based on price, location, and ratings.

Problem Statement:

The process of diagnosing an issue with your car and getting it fixed it long, costly, and not enjoyable. It can be difficult to know exaclty how to solve an issue without spending hundreds of dollars on a simple mechanic visit.

Research:

Extensive research was done for this project over the course of a 4 weeks along each stage of building our application. Esentially, we had 3 stages of research to test various aspects of our application and design systems.
1. Use research to define the users We began the project by collectively asking 5+ potential users about the concept for our project.
Do you own a car?
How old are you?
How important is it to your daily routine to have a car reliably? (1-5)
What did you do the last time your car broke down?
How often does your car break down often?
Do you have any idea when you will need service on your car next?
What were major pain points or bottlenecks with your experience the last time your car broke down and needed to be repaired?
How would you feel if there was an app that predictively analyzes data from the car and driving patterns to predict if something has started to go wrong?
These questions helped us define a few different "personas" or personality types that would use our app. The two types we defined we the casual user, someone that drives to get from A to B with little knowledge of cars, and the car enthusiast, someone that loves cars and knows them intimately.

2. The second stage of research was to display our Visual Value Proposition version 1 and app mock ups. We received over 40 responses on our Qualtrics survey. Below are images of the survey.

Main Points:

We learned that our Visual Value Proposition was too focused on one audience. It was not generizable and the users did not connect with it. Most said they enjoyed the text on it because it explained well what the product does, however. We also learned that our Mockups were far too incomplete and not ready to be shown. We needed to clean them up a lot to make them more understandable.

3. The final stage of research was to present our nearly complete product to our mentors. We went through our visual value proposition and presented our app mockups. Our VVP was still a bit too targeted for the serious users, rather than the general audience. Also, the flow of our presentation was not where it needed it to be. We fixed the order of our project as well as the look of our VVP.
