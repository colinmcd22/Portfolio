LavaLab is a student-run incubator program that takes a cohort of about 28 students each semester and teaches them how to create and execute an idea. Each team has a product manager, two developers, and a designer. I was a Product Manager for about 4 months for my team. Our app was called Croo, a way to foster organic relationships and community within a company. At the end of the semester, each team selects one person to pitch their idea in front of an audience and judges. As the product manager, I was tasked with pitching the idea and our team won the People's Choice Award!

Process:

The semester was segmented into different parts of the application we needed to execute on. We first

  1. Found a problem
  2. Brainstormed solutions
  3. Conducted market research
  4. Designed the application
  5. Coded the application
  6. Created pitch presentation

Problem Statement:

Companies struggle to create a welcoming community for their employees, leaving them to feel isolated within their own workplace. The culture within a company is vital to keep employees motivated, happy, and working hard. But it can be difficult to foster a community like this without a lot of intervention.


Croo's Solution:

Croo fosters company culture by facilitating the creation of organic relationships. Our app uses a reward system and AI learning to pair coworkers together for a coffee, lunch, or a hangout. Each time employees meet up and do an activity, their network within the company visually grows in the app and they are one step closer to a reward.

Final Pitch Deck:

The pitch deck for Croo turned out great. We really focused on showing the audience how exactly we foster a stronger company culture. We walked them through the app step by step and displayed each vital feature.

Reflection:

This experience taught me a lot about how to work with a team. There were many instances where I had to step in to resolve a conflict, motivate a fellow founder, or make a tough decision. This semester was not an easy one as I spent over 3 hours every day after classes working through the app. We had many different pivots and changes, but in the end my team and I executed very well. I also learned a lot about conducting market research, receiving feedback on ideas, and Figma. Overall, we were able to build a successful app and present it to a group of people that loved it!
